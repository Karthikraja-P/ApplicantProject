Behavioural Games Assessment

2 games · 5 minutes each · 10 minutes total

Why Games?
Traditional tests ask what you know, but games show how you think under pressure and uncertainty. These assessments identify your unique cognitive style—how you manage risk, detect patterns, and balance effort vs reward.

Unlike multiple-choice tests, these games capture your natural decision-making process. Play naturally and follow your intuition. There are no "right" or "wrong" personalities, only different strategies that reflect your mental strengths.

🎈 Game 1 — Balloon Game (BART)

Measures: Risk Appetite & Reward Sensitivity
The **Balloon Analogue Risk Task (BART)** is a widely respected cognitive assessment used to understand how you navigate risk and reward in real-time.

In this game, you earn virtual money by pumping a balloon. Every pump increases its value, but also the risk of it bursting. If it pops, you lose everything for that specific balloon.

**How it works:**
  • Click "Pump" to inflate the balloon. Each pump adds **$0.05** to your potential earnings.
  • Click "Collect & Bank" at any time to secure your earnings for that round.
  • If you pump too much and it pops, you earn **$0.00** for that round.

Your goal is to maximize your **Total Banked** amount over 15 balloons. There is no single "best" pump number; we are looking for how consistently you stick to your chosen risk strategy.
Strategy Tip: Pay attention to the point at which balloons tend to pop. Is it better to go for the "big win" or take many "small wins"?

🃏 Game 2 — Cards Game (Iowa Gambling Task)

Measures: Pattern Recognition & Decision Learning
The **Iowa Gambling Task (IGT)** simulates real-world decision-making under uncertainty. It measures your ability to identify patterns and learn which choices provide long-term benefits versus short-term lures.

You will see 4 decks of cards (A, B, C, and D). Some decks are "good" over the long run, while others are "bad" (they offer big wins but even bigger losses).

**How it works:**
  • Click on **any deck** to draw a card.
  • Each card will either add to or subtract from your total balance.
  • You start with **$2,000**. Your goal is to end with as much as possible.

Don't try to calculate the math—follow your gut! As you play, you'll start to feel which decks are friendlier than others.
Careful: Some decks might look very profitable at first but hide massive penalties later. Only experience will reveal the truth!
